01 / THE BASICS

What is Discord?

Think of Discord as a clubhouse for your study group. It's a free app where people chat by typing, talk out loud in voice rooms, and share files and links, all in one place.

Our clubhouse is called a server. Inside it are channels, which are like separate rooms, each for a different purpose. One room is for questions, one is for resources, one is for talking out loud, and so on. You'll get the hang of it fast.

No pressure

You don't need to understand everything before you start. Just get in, say hi, and learn as you go. Everyone here was new once.

02 / GETTING IN

Get an account & join

You can use Discord three ways: in a web browser (nothing to install), as a desktop app (Windows/Mac), or as a phone app (iPhone/Android). Pick whatever's easiest, you can use more than one, and your account syncs across all of them.

◆ Web
◆ Desktop app
◆ Phone app

Step 1 — Create your free account

  1. Web: go to discord.com and click Login, then Register. Phone: install "Discord" from the App Store or Google Play and tap Register.
  2. Enter your email (or phone number), pick a username and password, and add your birthday.
  3. Check your email for a verification message from Discord and click the link inside. That confirms your account.

Step 2 — Join the kappa server

  1. Click the invite link your organizer sent you (it looks like discord.gg/xxxxx).
  2. It opens Discord and shows an Accept Invite button. Click it.
  3. You're in! 🎉 You'll see the server appear on the left edge of your screen.
If the link doesn't work

Invite links can expire for safety. If yours says "invalid or expired," just ask your organizer for a fresh one. It's normal, not something you did wrong.

03 / THE LAYOUT

The screen, explained

When you open the server, the screen has three main parts. Once you know these, you'll never feel lost.

◧
Far left — server list
A vertical strip of circle icons. Each circle is a different server you've joined. Our kappa server lives here, click its icon to enter it.
☰
Middle-left — channel list
All the rooms inside the server. Channels with a # are text rooms (you type). Channels with a 🔊 are voice rooms (you talk). Click any channel to open it.
▭
Center — the conversation
The big area where messages appear. At the bottom is the box where you type. This is where most of the action happens.
Categories

You'll notice the channel list has bold gray headings like INFO and STEADY. Those are just labels that group related rooms together, you can't click into them to chat. The actual rooms are the # and 🔊 channels underneath. On phone, tap the heading to expand or collapse a group.

05 / TALKING (BY TYPING)

Sending messages

Everything happens in the message box at the bottom of a text channel. Here's the handful of things worth knowing.

Send a message

Click the box at the bottom, type, and press Enter (or tap the send arrow on phone). That's it. To add a new line without sending, press Shift + Enter.

Reply to someone

Hover over their message (or long-press on phone) and choose Reply. Your message will link back to theirs so everyone knows what you're responding to.

Mention someone

Type @ followed by their name and pick them from the list. They'll get a notification. Use this when you're talking to a specific person, but don't overdo it.

React with an emoji

Hover a message and click the little smiley face to add a reaction. A quick 👍 or 🎉 is a friendly, low-effort way to respond.

Share code or a link

Just paste a link and it'll preview automatically. For code, wrap it in three backticks on the line before and after, like ```, and it'll show in a clean code block that's easy to read.

Your first message

Head to #kappa right now and say hi. Tell the pod your name and one thing you're hoping to get out of studying together. It breaks the ice for everyone.

06 / VOICE

Using voice (🔊 Live)

Voice chat sounds intimidating but it's the easiest thing here. It's just hopping on a call with your pod.

  1. Click the 🔊 Live channel. On desktop/web you join instantly. On phone, tap it then tap Join Voice.
  2. The first time, your browser or phone may ask permission to use your microphone, tap Allow.
  3. You're in the call. A small panel shows who else is here. Talk normally, others will hear you.
  4. Use the mute button (microphone icon) when you're not talking, it cuts background noise for everyone.
  5. To leave, click the hang-up icon (phone with an X). You can rejoin anytime.

Sharing your screen

Want to show the pod what you're working on? In the voice call, click Share Your Screen (a monitor icon). You can share your whole screen or just one window. Great for walking through a problem together. (Screen share works on desktop and web; on phone it's more limited.)

Camera is optional

You never have to turn on your camera. Voice-only is completely normal and most people study that way. Join, listen, talk when you want.

07 / ETIQUETTE

How to be a great podmate

A study pod works because people show up for each other. None of this is hard, it's mostly just being kind and asking good questions.

Do

  • Ask questions, even "basic" ones. Someone else is wondering the same thing.
  • Share what's working for you, your notes help others.
  • Be encouraging when a podmate is stuck.
  • Use Reply so people know which message you mean.
  • Celebrate small wins. Studying is a grind, cheer each other on.

Try not to

  • Say "just google it." We help each other here.
  • Mass-mention people with @ unless it's important.
  • Post huge walls of text, break it into smaller messages.
  • Feel shy about not knowing something. Nobody's judging.

Asking a good question

The fastest way to get help: say what you're trying to do, what you expected, and what actually happened. "How do I study X" is fine, but "I'm on topic X and confused about Y, here's what I tried" gets you a much better answer.

What's a thread?

Sometimes a question grows into a long back-and-forth. To keep the main chat tidy, you can hover a message and choose Create Thread, a little side-conversation branches off. You don't need to use threads to start, but now you know what they are when you see one.

08 / STUDY TIPS

Making the most of your pod

Your pod is your biggest advantage. Here's how to actually use it.

Check in regularly

Pop into #kappa daily, even just to say what you studied. Small, steady check-ins keep you accountable and keep the pod alive.

Study together in 🔊 Live

You don't have to study alone. Jump into 🔊 Live with podmates, even silently working "alongside" others (sometimes called a body-doubling or co-working session) helps you focus.

Build the resource library

Found a helpful article, video, or explanation? Post it in #resources so the whole pod benefits. Teaching and sharing is one of the best ways to lock in what you've learned.

Ask early, ask often

Don't sit stuck for hours. Drop your question in #kappa and keep moving. A pod that asks questions freely learns faster than one that suffers in silence.

You've got this

Showing up is most of the battle. Get in, say hi, ask your first question, and let the pod carry you forward. See you in #kappa. 🎓
